Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

A helmet is considered old and needs replacing if it shows visible signs of wear and tear like cracks in the shell, loose padding, frayed straps, significant dents, fading colors, or if it's older than five years, regardless of its appearance, as most safety organizations recommend replacing helmets after that timeframe due to potential degradation of internal materials even without visible damage.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that has been in storage, perform a thorough inspection to ensure it's safe; this includes checking tire pressure, brake function, chain lubrication, and overall condition for rust or damage, as prolonged storage can lead to issues like flat tires, corroded components, and potentially faulty brakes; it's best to take it for a short test ride around the block before committing to a longer ride.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

always wear a helmet, ride with traffic flow, use hand signals when turning or stopping, stay aware of your surroundings, yield to pedestrians, ride in a predictable manner, check your blind spots before changing lanes, and follow all local traffic laws applicable to cyclists
